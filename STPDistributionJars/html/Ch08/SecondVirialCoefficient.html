Program SecondVirialCoefficient

Approximation techniques are used to study the effect of inter-particle interactions in gases. A common technique is the virial density expansion. The first-order expansion for the ideal gas is given by

    PV/NkT = 1 + ρ B2(T),     (1)

where the second virial coefficient is

  B2(T) = 2π ∫ [1 - eu(r)/kT] r2 dr     (2)

where the integral runs from 0 to ∞ and u(r) is a spherically symmetric inter-particle potential.

Program SecondVirialCoefficient computes B2(T) for the Lennard-Jones potential.

Problem: Temperature dependence of B2

  1. A potential that captures some of the properties of the Lennard-Jones potential is the square well potential which is defined as

     u(r)= ∞   (r < σ),

             -ε   (σ < r < λσ),

             0   (r > λσ),         (3)

    where σ is the diameter of the hard core, λσ is the range of the attractive well, and ε is the well depth. Show that for this potential the Mayer function f(r) is given by

     f(r)= -1   (r < σ),

             g   (σ < r < λσ),

             0   (r > λσ),        (4)

    and

    B2(T) = (2πσ3/3)[1 - (λ3 - 1)g].    (5)

    where g=eβε - 1.

  2. Plot the temperature dependence of B2(T) as given by Eq. (2) and compare it to the approximate temperature dependence given by B2 = b - a/kT.
  3. Program SecondVirialCoefficient evaluates the integral over r in Eq. (2) numerically to determine the temperature dependence of B2 for the Lennard-Jones potential. Compare the numerical results for the Lennard-Jones potential with the approximate result B2 = b - a/kT. At what temperature does B2 vanish? How does this temperature compare with that predicted by B2 = b - a/kT = 0?
