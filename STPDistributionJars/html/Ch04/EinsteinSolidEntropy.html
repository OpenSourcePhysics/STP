Program EinsteinSolidEntropy

The Einsein model consists of N oscillators and E packets of energy which can be distributed among the oscillators in Ω ways, where Ω = (E+N-1)!/[E! (N-1)!]. Each way is a microstate.

Program EinsteinSolidEntropy considers two systems which can share energy; the number of oscillators in each system is fixed. The key idea is to explore the total entropy S = k ln Ω and the inverse temperature ∂S/∂E for each system at equilibrium. In the program k = 1.

Problem: Two Einstein solids in thermal contact

The entropies of two Einstein solids in thermal contact are computed and plotted in Program EinsteinSolidEntropy.
  1. Consider NA = 50, NB = 30, and total energy Etot = 200. Discuss the qualitative dependence of SA, SB, and Stot on the energy EA. Why is SA is an increasing function of EA and SB a decreasing function of EA? Given this dependence of SA and SB on EA, why does Stot have a maximum at a particular value of EA?
  2. The values of the inverse of ∂SA/∂EA and ∂SA/∂EA are shown in the lower right corner by clicking on the corresponding plots. What is the relation of these values of the inverse of the slopes to the temperature? Determine the value of EA at which the slopes are equal. What can you say about the total entropy at this value of EA? Consider several values of NA, NB ≠ NA and Etot.
